Hands-On ▾
Selling & Copywriting
The gap most artists never close. You can have the best music, best Patreon, and best beats — and still make nothing if you can't write copy that moves people to act or hold a sales conversation with a label.
The Four Places Selling Shows Up for Rokki
  • Email sequences — your welcome series, Patreon launch email, and release announcements are all sales copy. They need a hook, a story, and a clear ask.
  • Beat & sync descriptions — every listing on BeatStars or Musicbed is a micro sales page. Vivid, artist-focused, one sentence that makes someone hear the song before they press play.
  • Patreon & offers — lead with the identity and belonging ("be part of what I'm building"), then the deliverables. Sell the feeling, back it up with the facts.
  • Label negotiations — know your walk-away number, your ideal terms, and your BATNA before every conversation. Never negotiate without those three written down.
Core Skills to Build (in order)
  • Phase 1: Learn StoryBrand — your fan/buyer is the hero, you're the guide. Apply this to every email and caption you write from now on.
  • Phase 2: Learn the hook formula — every piece of copy needs one. Problem → agitate → solve, or desire → obstacle → resolution. Claude can help you apply both.
  • Phase 3: Learn negotiation basics — BATNA, anchoring, silence as a tool. Apply to label deals, booking fees, and any paid collaboration.
Claude Can Help You With
For any offer or copy task, ask Claude: "Rewrite this [email/caption/Patreon description/beat listing] using the StoryBrand framework. The reader is an artist/fan who wants [X]. I'm offering [Y]. The call to action is [Z]. Make it feel human, not corporate."

For negotiation prep: "I'm about to negotiate a record deal with [label]. My ideal outcome: [describe]. My walk-away point: [describe]. Role-play as the A&R and give me a realistic opening offer. Help me prepare my counter."

Mar 2026 – Jun 2027
Revenue Plan

$4K/mo post-tax by Dec 2026. $10K/mo post-tax by Jun 2027. This isn't one big income source — it's six streams that each contribute a piece, timed to when you'll realistically have the audience and catalog to activate them.

The honest math upfront Streaming alone won't get you there. 100K streams on Spotify pays roughly $400–500. That's a foundation, not a business. The $4K target requires stacking multiple streams — streaming royalties, label advances, sync licensing, beat/sample sales, direct fan support, and live/DJ bookings. Some of these activate early, some require you to build an audience first. The plan below sequences them in order of what you can realistically start when.
The 6 Revenue Streams — Sequenced by When to Activate
01
Beat Sales & Stem Packs
Start: April 2026  ·  Platform: BeatStars, Airbit, Gumroad
target by Dec
$500–800/mo
Why start here first

You're already making music. Packaging that production as a product requires almost no extra work — and it starts generating income before you have a significant audience. Even 5–10 beat sales/month at $30–100 each adds up fast.

How to execute
  • Set up a BeatStars Pro page ($20/mo) — upload 10–20 beats or instrumentals from your catalog
  • Price leases at $30–50, exclusives at $200–500. Offer stem packs for $75–150.
  • Post "beat tape" content on TikTok/IG — producers and artists are actively searching for this
  • Every flip you make? Upload the instrumental. It's already done — just package it.
  • By end of 2026 goal: 20+ beats listed, generating passive sales from your social traffic
Ask Claude: "Write 10 TikTok captions for beat/instrumental videos. I produce [genre] music. The captions should appeal to artists looking for beats and drive them to my BeatStars page. Make them sound like a producer, not a salesperson."
📖 Selling skill — beat page copy: Write a one-sentence description for every beat that paints a picture of the song it could become ("dark 808s for a late-night confessional"). List your most expensive exclusive first — price anchoring makes leases feel like a deal by comparison. Use Claude: "Write sales descriptions for these 10 beats. Genre: [X]. Make each one 1–2 sentences, vivid, artist-focused."
02
Sync Licensing
Start: May 2026  ·  Platform: Musicbed, Artlist, Pond5, Epidemic Sound
target by Dec
$300–600/mo
Why this is pure passive income

You upload your catalog once. When a YouTuber, brand, filmmaker, or creator uses your track — you get paid. No additional work. By May you'll have 5+ originals and 2+ flips registered. That catalog grows every month.

How to execute
  • Register with Musicbed (quality bar is high but pays well — $50–500 per license) and/or Artlist
  • Also submit to Pond5 and AudioJungle for broader reach at lower price points
  • Ensure you own 100% of your masters before registering — no samples in the tracks
  • Register all tracks with a PRO (ASCAP, BMI, or SOCAN) to collect performance royalties on top
  • Goal: 20+ tracks registered across 2 platforms by Sep 2026. Income is slow at first, compounds by 2027.
03
Streaming Royalties + PRO Income
Active: now  ·  Grows with: release cadence + playlist placements
target by Dec
$400–700/mo
The realistic numbers

Spotify pays ~$0.003–0.005/stream. 100K streams = $300–500. Add Apple Music ($0.01/stream), Tidal ($0.013), and YouTube, and your blended rate improves. PRO royalties (performance rights) add another layer on top — register every track before release.

How to maximise this
  • Register with a PRO (ASCAP or BMI in the US, SOCAN in Canada) immediately — they collect radio, streaming, and performance royalties
  • Use DistroKid's "Songwriter" add-on or similar to collect publishing royalties from mechanical rights
  • Playlist placements are the multiplier — one Spotify editorial placement can add 20–50K streams on a single track
  • Catalog matters: 24 releases by Dec means 24 tracks earning simultaneously. By 2027 that tail income compounds significantly.
04
Direct Fan Support — Patreon / Bandcamp
Start: Sep 2026 (once you have an audience)  ·  Platform: Patreon, Bandcamp
target by Dec
$500–1,000/mo
Why September, not now

Patreon only works if people already care about you. Launch it too early and it looks empty and desperate. By September you'll have 6K+ followers across platforms, a real email list, and 12+ releases — that's an audience ready to support you.

How to structure it
  • Tier 1 — $5/mo: Early access to every release 1 week before it drops
  • Tier 2 — $15/mo: Early access + exclusive stems/loops + monthly Q&A
  • Tier 3 — $50/mo: Everything above + your personal beat feedback on 1 track/month
  • 100 fans at $5 = $500/mo. 50 fans at $15 = $750/mo. These are realistic by December with 6K+ followers.
  • Bandcamp: sell your catalog as downloads with "name your price" — even small amounts add up
Ask Claude in September: "Help me write my Patreon launch post and the descriptions for 3 membership tiers. I'm Rokki, I make [genre] music. I have [X] followers and [Y] email subscribers. I want tiers at $5, $15, and $50/month. Make it feel exclusive and personal, not transactional."
📖 Selling skill — Patreon launch: The single biggest mistake artists make launching Patreon is leading with what's in the tiers. Lead with why instead — why you're building this, what it means to you, what kind of relationship you want with your real fans. People don't pay $15/month for stems. They pay because they want to be part of something. Sell the belonging, not the deliverable. Then list the deliverables.
05
Label Advances
Start: Sep–Dec 2026 (when deals close)  ·  One-time but significant
typical range
$2–20K+/deal
What a label advance actually is

An advance is a loan against future royalties — not free money. The label recoups it from your earnings before you see royalties. Smaller well-known labels in your market might offer $2–10K for a single or EP deal. That said, even a $5K advance paid out at signing is real income that counts toward your monthly target that month.

What to negotiate for
  • Advance paid in two installments: 50% at signing, 50% on release — protects you if the label stalls
  • Short term: aim for 1–2 year deals, not multi-album 5-year deals at this stage
  • Retain as much of your masters as possible — a license deal is better than a full transfer for an emerging artist
  • Read Passman's chapter on record deals before you sit in any negotiation room
⚠️ Important: Don't rely on advances for monthly income — they're lumpy and unpredictable. Count them as a bonus that clears your month's target when they arrive, not as recurring revenue. The other 5 streams are your foundation.
06
Live Performance & DJ Bookings
Start: anytime — but grows significantly with audience size
target by Dec
$500–1,500/mo
The compounding effect

Every show you play also generates streams — people Shazam your set, follow you afterward, and share clips. Bookings are income AND marketing simultaneously. Even 2 shows/month at $300–750 each = $600–1,500/mo with zero overhead.

How to grow bookings
  • Start local: approach bars, clubs, events, and promoters in your market directly — don't wait for inbound
  • Create a simple booking press kit (1-page PDF): your bio, your music, your social stats, your rate card
  • Post set or performance content on TikTok — promoters book artists they've seen online performing
  • As your follower count grows, your rate grows. 10K followers = significantly easier booking conversations
